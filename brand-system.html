A· — a small mark, with a signal.

A wordmark and a single terracotta dot. The dot is the “on” — every smart system starts with a signal. Hawaii enters the brand through a plumeria pattern that lives in the background, never on the mark itself. This keeps Aloha unmistakable next to Musubi’s blue, and unmistakably not a generic merchant-services logo.

05 — Brand voice · vs the payment processor

A reminder of how the brand should read next to a typical Hawaii merchant-services competitor. Same warmth, different altitude.

Aloha Smart System
We process your credit cards.
Then we build the system on top.
Built by operators. Multi-vendor settlement, kiosk, KDS, pickup TVs, marketing — engineered on Clover, integrated with Zorder.

“Aloha ‘ohana — your trusted local payments partner since 2007.”
Hibiscus, rainbow, family. Sells card processing + Clover hardware. No multi-vendor system, no KDS, no pickup TV, no integrated marketing.
06 — Do’s & don’ts
Do
  • Keep the dot one color: terracotta on light, paper on dark/terra.
  • Use plumeria pattern only as background texture — backs of cards, footers, decorative panels.
  • Pair the mark with generous whitespace; let the dot breathe.
  • When you need “hawaii signal” in copy areas, use the Built in 808 badge, not flowers on the mark.
Don’t
  • Don’t add a hibiscus, leaf, wave, or sunburst onto the wordmark.
  • Don’t recolor the dot to the gold or palm green.
  • Don’t stack the mark on a busy photo without the paper card backdrop.
  • Don’t use both terracotta and Musubi blue in the same composition — pick a side.
